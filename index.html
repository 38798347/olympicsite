Olympic Webpage Project

Rifle Shooting

Description: Olympic shooting is a precision sport where athletes compete to hit targets with firearms, testing their accuracy, focus, and technique

Rules:

 Disciplines: Rifle, Pistol, and Shotgun.
Scoring: Based on accuracy; hit targets to score points.
Format: Qualifying rounds lead to finals.
Shooting Positions: Varies by event—standing, prone, or kneeling.
Targets: Fixed or moving, depending on the discipline.

Fencing

Description: There are 12 events in Olympic fencing, with three types of weapon used: foil, épée and sabre.

Rules:

Scoring: Points for touches on valid areas; first to a set number or most points wins.
Right of Way: In Foil and Sabre, the attacker has priority if both score simultaneously.
Gear: Full protective gear is required.
Match Format: Three periods of three minutes or until one fencer scores 15 points.
Penalties: Yellow (warning), Red (point loss), Black (disqualification)

Archery

Description: involves shooting arrows at a target 70 meters away. The target has 10 concentric rings, with the innermost circle worth 10 points. Archers compete individually or in teams, aiming to score the highest points

Rules:

Rounds: Archers shoot 6 arrows per set in a match.
Scoring: The highest score per arrow is 10 points.
Match Format: Individual matches are best of 5 sets; first to 6 points wins. Team matches are based on the total score over 4 ends
Tie: In a tie, a single arrow shoot-off decides the winner, with the closest to the center winning.

Weight Lifting

Description involves lifting the heaviest weights in two styles: the Snatch and the Clean & Jerk. Athletes compete in different weight categories.

Rules:

Lifts: Each athlete gets 3 attempts in both Snatch and Clean & Jerk.
Scoring: The best successful lift in each style is combined for a total score.
Winning: The athlete with the highest total score in their weight category wins.
Tie: In case of a tie, the lifter with the lower body weight wins.
